Features

Most founders try to validate by posting on social media and hoping something goes viral — a slow, expensive gamble that reaches whoever happens to be scrolling, not whoever actually has the problem. Facade skips the gamble: intent search finds the exact people already describing your problem, with far greater accuracy and far fewer resources spent chasing an audience, so testing an idea stays as cheap and fast as it should be.

AI problem mapping

Facade reads your idea and reverse-engineers it into the pain it removes, not the product it is. That reframing matters: almost nobody searches Reddit for a product that doesn't exist yet, but plenty of people are already describing the problem it would fix.

Reddit intent detection

We scan relevant subreddits and rank threads and comments by purchase intent — phrases like "I wish there was a tool for this" or someone actively comparing workarounds. That narrows millions of posts down to the small slice that's a genuine warm lead.

Semi-automatic outreach

Each high-intent match gets a short, personal reply referencing their specific post, not a templated pitch. You review and approve every message before it sends, so outreach stays within subreddit rules and reads like a person, not a bot.

Instant landing pages

Your idea becomes a live, credible landing page in seconds, with real pricing and a real call to action, so the people you reach have somewhere to click, not just another survey link.

Conversion tracking

Every view, click, and add-to-cart is logged and tied back to the exact subreddit, thread, and message that drove it, so you know precisely which pain point and phrasing actually turns strangers into buyers.

Post-click surveys

When someone tries to buy something that isn't built yet, we don't just say sorry — we ask what they expected to get and what price felt fair. One answer like that often tells you more than a hundred cold signups.
